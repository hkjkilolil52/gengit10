An internet search produced this definition of cryptocurrency: “a digital currency in which encryption techniques are used to regulate the generation of units of currency and verify the transfer of funds, operating independently of a central bank.” That definition alone probably best explains why you should hire the professionals at Elite Crypto Consulting to introduce you to this complex form of currency.

While most Americans are not intimately familiar with cryptocurrencies, Bitcoin has managed to make a substantial name for itself following its dynamic performance history, making it the most popular among the various currencies. And for all the buzz in the financial world, there is a very small percentage of investors who have actually purchased cryptocurrencies.

A recent survey attempted to discover the primary reasons American investors were not purchasing more Bitcoin and its crypto cousins, Bitcoin cash, Ethereum, Stellar Lumens, Ripple and Cardano. The three most common explanations for not investing in crypto were given as: no interest or need, too risky of an investment, and too complicated.

Of those who have purchased cryptocurrency, the majority did so after having done their own research rather than rely on each currency’s social media influence. Among those professing to own cryptocurrencies, millennials are at the top, followed by Gen Xers and last, but not least, baby boomers. Of those who plan to purchase crypto in the future, the rankings remain the same.

While many are talking about crypto, many are still very unfamiliar with it.
